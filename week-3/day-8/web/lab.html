Beacon Retail Group — Job Posting

Your Evaluator agent needs this job description to score candidates against. Copy it into your Evaluator's system prompt or context.

Seasonal Retail Associate

Position: Seasonal Retail Associate
Company: Beacon Retail Group
Locations: Multiple stores across the region (12 hiring locations)
Duration: May - August 2026 (with potential extension for strong performers)
Hours: 25-40 hours/week, must be available weekends and at least 2 holidays
Pay: $16-$19/hour based on experience

Key Responsibilities:
- Operate POS register and process customer transactions
- Assist customers with product location, sizing, and recommendations
- Stock shelves, maintain displays, and organize backroom inventory
- Handle returns and exchanges per Beacon's return policy
- Maintain clean and organized store environment
- Support seasonal promotions and sale events

Required Qualifications:
- 18+ years old
- Available for the full May-August season (partial availability considered for strong candidates)
- Able to stand for extended periods, lift up to 30 lbs
- Reliable transportation to assigned store location
- High school diploma or equivalent

Preferred Qualifications:
- Previous retail or customer service experience
- POS/cash register experience
- Bilingual (English + Spanish, Mandarin, or other)
- Open availability including evenings, weekends, and holidays
- Experience with inventory management or visual merchandising
- First aid or safety certification

Part 1: Build the Parser Agent

~30 minutes | AI Studio

What the Parser Does

The Parser takes raw resume text and extracts structured data. It does NOT evaluate quality — it just organizes the information.

Scaffold Prompt (click to expand)
You are a resume parser for Beacon Retail Group's hiring pipeline. Your job: Extract structured data from raw resume text. Do NOT evaluate or judge the candidate — just extract the facts. For each resume, output a JSON object with these fields: { "candidate_id": "[C-XX from the first line]", "name": "[full name]", "contact": { "email": "[if provided]", "phone": "[if provided]", "city": "[city, state]" }, "work_experience": [ { "title": "[job title]", "company": "[company name]", "location": "[city, state]", "start_date": "[start]", "end_date": "[end or Present]", "duties": ["[duty 1]", "[duty 2]"] } ], "education": [ { "degree": "[degree or diploma]", "institution": "[school name]", "graduation": "[date or expected]" } ], "skills": ["[skill 1]", "[skill 2]"], "certifications": ["[cert 1]"], "availability": "[full text of availability statement]", "references": "[what they said about references]" } IMPORTANT: - The resume ID is on the first line in [C-XX] format. ALWAYS include it. - Extract dates exactly as written (don't convert formats). - If a section is missing, use null or empty array. - Output valid JSON only — no commentary before or after.

Test Your Parser

  1. Open AI Studio
  2. Paste the scaffold prompt (or write your own) as the system instruction
  3. Set temperature to 0.1-0.2 (for consistent JSON output)
  4. Copy a practice resume from below and paste it as user input
  5. Verify the output is valid JSON with all expected fields
  6. Test with 3-5 different practice resumes, including varied formats

Compare with Classmates

Did your Parser extract the same fields? Did anyone find a resume that breaks the Parser? Share your trickiest edge case.

Part 2: Build the Evaluator Agent

~30 minutes | AI Studio

What the Evaluator Does

The Evaluator takes Parser output + the job posting and scores each candidate. This is where your team's judgment matters most.

Output Format Template (click to expand) — You write the scoring criteria!
You are a candidate evaluator for Beacon Retail Group's seasonal hiring. You will receive a parsed resume (JSON) and the job posting. Score the candidate and identify any concerns. Output this JSON structure for each candidate: { "candidate_id": "[from input]", "name": "[from input]", "score": [0-100], "category": "Strong" | "Moderate" | "Weak", "evaluation": { "experience_score": [0-25, your reasoning], "availability_score": [0-20, your reasoning], "customer_service_score": [0-15, your reasoning], "reliability_score": [0-15, your reasoning], "skills_match_score": [0-15, your reasoning] }, "red_flags": [ {"flag": "[description]", "severity": "minor" | "disqualifying"} ], "strengths": ["[strength 1]", "[strength 2]"], "concerns": ["[concern 1]"] } YOUR JOB: Define how you score each dimension. What makes a 25/25 on experience vs. a 10/25? That's YOUR team's scoring rubric. The job posting for reference: [PASTE THE JOB POSTING HERE]

Note: Unlike the Parser, you only get the output format — not the scoring logic. Your team decides how to weight experience, availability, skills, etc. This is where your pipeline will differ from other teams.

Calibrate Your Evaluator

  1. Run your Evaluator on 3 practice resumes (one strong, one moderate, one weak)
  2. Check: Does the strong candidate score significantly higher than the weak one?
  3. Adjust your scoring criteria until the results make sense
  4. Test with a red-flag resume — does your Evaluator catch the issue?

Compare with Classmates

Compare scores for the same candidate. If your Evaluator gives P-01 a 92 and theirs gives 75, discuss why — different rubrics produce different rankings.

Part 3: Build the Ranker Agent

~25 minutes | AI Studio

What the Ranker Does

The Ranker takes all Evaluator outputs (batch input) and produces a final ranked list, identifies patterns across candidates, and generates the competition submission JSON.

No scaffold prompt for this one. By now you should be able to write it from scratch. Think about:

  • What criteria does the Ranker use to break ties?
  • Should the Ranker look for patterns across resumes? (Hint: yes, for bonus points!)
  • Make sure the output matches the competition submission format
Pro Tip: Your Ranker should output the competition JSON structure directly. Check the Competition page for the exact format — top_10_hire, bottom_5, flags, bias_pairs, patterns.

Part 4: Wire the Pipeline in ADK

~30 minutes | ADK Visual Builder

Connect Your Agents

  1. Open the ADK Visual Builder
  2. Create 3 agents using your finalized prompts from AI Studio
  3. Wire them in sequence: Parser → Evaluator → Ranker
  4. Test with a single practice resume end-to-end
  5. Then test with 5+ practice resumes to verify consistency
Troubleshooting: If the pipeline breaks between agents, the data contract is usually the issue. Check that your Parser output exactly matches what your Evaluator expects.

Compare with Classmates

How long does your pipeline take per resume? Compare processing times. Faster pipelines will have more time for iteration on Day 9.
