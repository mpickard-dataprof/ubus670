Capstone Grading Rubric

Days 8-9: AI Resume Screening Competition — 20% of Course Grade

Part 1: Competition Score (100 points)

Your team's best submission (out of 3 attempts) is auto-scored against the ground truth answer key.

Dimension Points How It's Scored
Top 10 Ranking 30 Set membership (25 pts): 2.5 pts per correct candidate in your top 10.
Position accuracy (5 pts): +5 if all top 3 exact, +2 if 2 exact, +1 if 1 exact.
Bottom 5 Identification 10 Strict (2 pts each): Candidate is in the true bottom 5.
Partial (1 pt each): Candidate is in positions 23-27 (close, but not bottom 5).
Max 10 pts.
Red Flag Detection + Severity 30 14 flags hidden across the resume set. For each:
Full credit (~2.1 pts): Correct candidate + flag described + correct severity.
Partial (1.0 pt): Correct candidate + flag described + wrong/missing severity.
Bias Pair Detection 15 5 candidate pairs with nearly identical qualifications. For each:
Full credit (3 pts): Both candidates identified + observation explaining similarity.
Partial (1 pt): One candidate + valid bias observation.
Pattern Discovery 15 3 hidden cross-resume patterns. For each:
Full credit (5 pts): At least 2 candidates identified + pattern described.
Must reference 2+ candidates to show the cross-resume connection.
Total 100
Attempts: Each team gets 3 submission attempts. Only your highest score counts. Use early attempts to identify gaps, then refine your pipeline and resubmit.

Part 2: Video Presentation (Separate Grade)

10-minute maximum team video, submitted to Blackboard by the posted deadline.

Section Time What to Cover
Architecture Overview ~1.5 min Your 3-agent pipeline design. What does each agent do? How do they pass data?
Results Defense ~3 min Your top 10 recommendations and why. Defend your rankings with specific evidence from resumes.
Obstacles + AI Help ~2 min What problems did you hit? How did AI help you overcome them? Show iteration, not perfection.
Hidden Patterns ~2 min What cross-resume patterns, bias pairs, or red flags did your team discover?
Lessons Learned ~1.5 min Coolest thing you built or learned. What would you do differently with more time?

Part 3: Submission Package (to Blackboard)

  • Video presentation (10 min max, MP4 or link to recording)
  • All agent system prompts (your "code" — the prompts for Parser, Evaluator, Ranker, and any stretch agents)
  • Pipeline architecture diagram (screenshot or drawing showing how agents connect)
  • Competition results JSON (copy of your final submission from the competition page)
  • Reflection document: obstacles faced, iterations tried, what you would improve

Reference: Red Flag Types

Flag Type Severity What to Look For
Fake institution Disqualifying Degree from a school/program that does not exist
Fabricated employer Disqualifying Work history at a company that does not exist (not just a small/closed business)
Contradictory dates Disqualifying Overlapping full-time employment at two jobs simultaneously
Self-contradictory claims Disqualifying Experience claims that are mathematically impossible given timeline
Inflated title Minor Impressive title at a very small operation (title doesn't match scope)
Unexplained gap >1 year Minor Large gap in employment with no explanation on the resume
Copy-paste work experience Minor Word-for-word identical job descriptions across different candidates/employers
Severity matters: Correctly identifying the flag is worth partial credit. Getting both the flag AND the correct severity earns full credit.
