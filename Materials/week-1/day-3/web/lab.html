Lab 3: Building Beacon's Knowledge Assistant

Transform company documents into an AI-powered knowledge base

Estimated time: 110 minutes

Lab Objectives

By completing this lab, you will:

  • Create a Gemini Gem with persistent Knowledge for Beacon Retail Group
  • Compare three context engineering approaches: no context, chat upload, and Gems
  • Test your AI assistant's ability to cite sources from uploaded documents
  • Stress-test cross-referencing, boundary detection, and ambiguity handling
  • Share your Gem with classmates and compare results

Tool Setup

What you need: A web browser and a free personal Google account.

0

Get Started with Google Gemini

You do NOT need a paid subscription. Gemini's free tier supports file uploads, Gems, and Knowledge uploads — everything needed for this lab. If prompted to select a model, choose the latest free-tier option (e.g., Gemini 2.5 Flash).
Context window limits: A context window is the amount of text an AI model can "remember" at once (similar to short-term memory). The free tier supports ~32K tokens (~24K words) in a chat conversation. The 5 Beacon documents total ~3,500 words, so they fit comfortably. For larger document sets, Google AI Studio (ai.google.dev) offers up to 1 million tokens for free.
Troubleshooting Common Issues:
  • File won't upload: Check that each file is under 10MB. Try .txt format instead of .pdf.
  • "Context limit reached": Start a new chat and re-upload. This is a real limitation worth noting!
  • AI gives generic answers (ignores your documents): Re-upload the files and explicitly say "Answer using ONLY the documents I uploaded."
  • Can't find the upload button: Look for a paperclip icon, "+" button, or "Attach files" near the text input area.

Part 1: Review & Prepare Documents

Time estimate: 15 minutes

Before building a knowledge assistant, take a few minutes to review the Beacon documents. Modern AI can read almost any format — even scanned PDFs and handwritten notes — but well-structured documents still help AI give better, more consistent answers.

1

Review the Documents

Skim through all 5 Beacon documents above. As you read, think about how well each one is organized.

Structure helps AI — but isn't required. AI can read messy documents just fine. But clear headings, labeled sections, and specific data points help AI give more precise answers. Think of it like an organized desk vs. a messy desk — you can find things either way, but organization is faster.

Part 2: Create Your Gemini Gem

Time estimate: 20 minutes

A Gemini Gem is a custom AI assistant you create inside Google Gemini. Unlike uploading documents to a regular chat (which disappears when the chat ends), a Gem keeps its Knowledge permanently and can be shared with others via a link.

3

Create Your Beacon Knowledge Gem

You'll create a Gem that acts as Beacon Retail Group's internal knowledge assistant.

You are Beacon Retail Group's internal knowledge assistant. You help the AI Strategy Task Force answer questions about the company. Rules: - Answer questions using ONLY the uploaded documents - Always cite which document your answer comes from (e.g., "According to the Q3 Financial Summary...") - If the answer isn't in the documents, say "I don't have that information in the uploaded documents" — do NOT guess - Be specific: use exact numbers, names, and dates from the documents
If your Gem doesn't have a "Knowledge" section: The Gems interface may vary slightly depending on your account. If you don't see a Knowledge upload option, try clicking "Edit" on your created Gem, or look for an "Add files" or "Upload" button within the Gem settings.
Why a Gem instead of regular chat? When you upload documents to a regular Gemini chat, the AI "forgets" them when you close the chat. A Gem's Knowledge is persistent — it stays available every time you open the Gem. This is a form of context engineering: designing how and when the AI gets information.

Compare with Classmates

Did your Gem instructions differ from your neighbor's? How might different instructions change the AI's behavior? You can share your Gem — click the three-dot menu (…) on your Gem and select "Share." Send the link to a classmate and see if they get the same answers you do.

Part 3: Stress-Testing Your Gem

Time estimate: 20 minutes

Now you'll test the limits of your Beacon Knowledge Gem by asking harder questions that require cross-referencing, boundary detection, and handling ambiguity.

5

Cross-Referencing Test

These questions require the Gem to combine information from MULTIPLE documents in its Knowledge.

Common AI challenge: Cross-referencing requires the AI to "remember" information from multiple documents simultaneously. Since your Gem has all 5 documents in its Knowledge, it should handle this well — but note any errors or inconsistencies.
6

Boundary Detection Test

These questions ask for information that ISN'T in the documents. A good knowledge assistant should say "I don't know" rather than making up information.

Why boundary detection matters: In business contexts, it's critical that AI doesn't fabricate information. A hallucinated financial figure or hiring statistic could lead to bad decisions. Your Gem's instructions ("If the answer isn't in the documents, say so") should help prevent this — this is an example of how context engineering improves AI reliability.
7

Ambiguity Handling Test

This question is intentionally vague and requires interpretation.

Ambiguity is common in business: Questions like "Should we expand?" or "Which initiative should we prioritize?" require judgment across competing stakeholder perspectives. AI can surface relevant information from each viewpoint, but humans must make the final call.

Compare with Classmates

Did your Gem recommend the same priority as your neighbor's? Try sharing your Gem link and asking the same question. Trade-off questions like this often produce different answers depending on the Gem's instructions and how the AI weighs evidence. This is exactly why humans must stay in the decision loop.

Context accumulation: Your Gem now has TWO types of context: (1) persistent Knowledge from the uploaded files, and (2) temporary context from this chat message. The persistent Knowledge stays forever; the chat message context disappears when you close this conversation. Understanding this distinction is key to effective context engineering.
