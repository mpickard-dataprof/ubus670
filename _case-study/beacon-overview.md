### Company History

Beacon Retail Group was founded in 1987 by Robert and Linda Marsh in Rockford, Illinois. What began as a single 8,000 square-foot store selling household goods, seasonal items, and local products has grown into a regional retail chain serving communities across the upper Midwest.

**Key Milestones:**

- **1987:** First store opens in Rockford, IL
- **1993:** Expansion to 5 stores; regional distribution center established
- **2001:** 15th store opens; company reaches $100M in annual revenue
- **2008:** Survived economic recession by focusing on value positioning
- **2015:** Second-generation leadership as founders' daughter Patricia Holloway becomes CEO
- **2019:** Launched e-commerce platform (beacon-retail.com)
- **2022:** Reached 25 stores; expanded into Indiana market
- **2024:** Began digital transformation initiative

### Business Model

Beacon Retail Group operates as a **regional general merchandise retailer**, competing in the space between national big-box stores (Walmart, Target) and smaller specialty retailers.

**Value Proposition:**
- **Community-focused:** Each store is tailored to local preferences
- **Quality at value:** Higher quality than dollar stores, more affordable than department stores
- **Personal service:** Knowledgeable staff, not just transactional checkout
- **Local sourcing:** 15% of products from regional suppliers

**Product Categories:**
1. Home & Kitchen (28% of revenue)
2. Seasonal & Holiday (22%)
3. Apparel & Accessories (18%)
4. Health & Beauty (12%)
5. Electronics & Office (10%)
6. Food & Grocery (10%)

### Competitive Landscape

**Direct Competitors:**
- Walmart (price leader, but impersonal)
- Target (style-focused, higher prices)
- Kohl's (apparel-focused)
- Regional grocery chains with general merchandise

**Competitive Advantages:**
1. Community relationships and local market knowledge
2. Flexible, responsive merchandising
3. Lower overhead than national chains
4. Established regional brand recognition

**Competitive Challenges:**
1. Limited e-commerce capabilities
2. Less buying power than national chains
3. Aging technology infrastructure
4. Difficulty attracting talent vs. larger employers
